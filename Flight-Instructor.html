Become a Flight Instructor with Crestwind Aviation

Join the Crestwind Aviation Club

At Crestwind Aviation, becoming a Certified Flight Instructor (CFI) is about joining a community of aviators and taking control of your learning journey. Our flying club offers a unique environment where you can develop the skills needed to teach others while benefiting from the camaraderie and support of fellow members.

Requirements to Become a CFI

  • Hold a Commercial Pilot Certificate with an Instrument Rating.
  • Pass the FAA Knowledge Tests: Fundamentals of Instructing (FOI) and Flight Instructor Airplane (FIA).
  • Log Required Flight Hours:
    • 250 hours total time
    • 15 hours as PIC in the aircraft category and class
  • Receive an Endorsement from an instructor for the CFI practical test.
  • Pass the CFI Practical Test (checkride) with an FAA Designated Pilot Examiner.

Syllabus: How the Club Prepares You

  • Training in Vintage Cessna 172s: Gain experience with traditional steam gauges, which are essential for teaching foundational flying skills.
  • Real-World Teaching Practice: In our club setting, you’ll practice instructional techniques with fellow members, simulating real instructional scenarios.
  • Scenario-Based Learning: Engage in flights that mirror real-world challenges you’ll face as an instructor.
  • Independent Instruction: Lead flights with club members acting as students, preparing you for the demands of a CFI.

Affordable and Practical Training

By training within our club, you access well-maintained aircraft at a reduced cost, making it easier to log the hours and gain the experience needed to become a CFI.

Ready to Start?

If you’re ready to take the next step toward becoming a flight instructor, Crestwind Aviation offers the environment and resources you need.
