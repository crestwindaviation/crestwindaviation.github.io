Purpose of Organization

  • Flight Training: Compliance with FAA Part 91 regulations.
  • Recreational Flying: Non-commercial aviation-related activities.
  • Cost Distribution: Facilitate access to aircraft while distributing ownership and training costs.

Club Membership

  • Membership Availability: Club Membership is available now at $11,000 per Membership. Contact now before they fill up.
  • Application: Membership is gained by purchasing a Membership from an existing member.
  • Membership Limits: Maximum of 8 members per aircraft.
  • Exchange Agreements: Members can exchange flight time between different aircraft, subject to insurance and mutual agreement.
  • Fees:
    • $500 non-refundable application fee.
    • Monthly membership fee: $700.
    • Flight rate: $35 per Tach hour (dry).

Funds and Fees

  • Monthly Fee Structure:
    • $250 to Manager.
    • $450 to maintenance fund.
    • Additional charges apply if fees are overdue.
  • Grounded Aircraft:
    • Monthly fees waived if grounded for over 30 days.
    • Monthly fees halved if grounded for 15 consecutive days.
  • Manager and Co-manager: Exempt from monthly dues but pay $35 per Tach hour for flight time.

Management Authority

  • Manager Role: Oversee business affairs, manage funds, handle scheduling, and ensure smooth operations.
  • Maintenance Decisions: Manager can propose aircraft upgrades and ground the aircraft for safety. Maintenance decisions may require a member vote.

Scheduling

  • Advance Scheduling: Members can schedule up to 2 weeks in advance, with a limit of 10 hours per week.
  • Overnight Trips: Requires a majority vote.
  • Emergency Landings: Expected at paved public airports unless an emergency dictates otherwise.

Voting and Decision-Making

  • Voting Rights: Each member's vote is proportional to their Membership.
  • Decision Types:
    • Majority Vote: Standard decisions, maintenance costs over $10,000.
    • Supermajority Vote: Significant changes affecting LLC assets, maintenance vetoes, grounding aircraft.

Aircraft Maintenance and Operations

  • Maintenance:
    • Performed by qualified members.
    • Logbooks managed by the Manager.
  • Post-Flight Requirements: Secure aircraft and report any discrepancies or damage.
  • Insurance: Liability insurance is mandatory; hull limit adjustments required for value increases.

Sale of Membership

  • Transfer Process: $500 fee for Membership transfers, subject to Manager or supermajority approval.
  • Valuation: Independent appraisal if Membership is unsold within 30 days.

Additional Aircraft and LLC Expansion

  • New Aircraft: Requires unanimous consent. New members can be added, with shared access to all aircraft.

Legal and Financial Protections

  • Indemnity: Members indemnify and hold harmless Crestwind Aviation LLC and other members.
  • Removal of Members: Grounds include non-payment, misuse of aircraft, and FAA violations, requiring a vote for removal.
  • Binding Agreement: This Agreement binds all members and their legal representatives.
