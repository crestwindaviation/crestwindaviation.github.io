Crestwind Aviation Club: Instrument Pilot Rating

Own the Skies, Master the Instruments

At Crestwind Aviation Club, becoming an instrument-rated pilot is about practical experience, community support, and mastering the essentials in a hands-on environment. Our club focuses on giving you the skills needed to confidently fly under IFR (Instrument Flight Rules) conditions, all while learning in a setting that emphasizes real-world application.

Membership is available today at $11,000. Contact us at crestwindaviation@gmail.com before they are gone.

Why Crestwind Aviation Club?

  • Cessna 172 Training: Fly and own a share in the iconic Cessna 172, the world’s most popular trainer. This consistency in aircraft builds familiarity and confidence.
  • Vintage Aircraft & Steam Gauges: Learn on traditional steam gauges in vintage aircraft. This experience sharpens your precision and situational awareness, essential skills for any instrument pilot.
  • Community Support: Join a network of pilots who are committed to helping each other succeed. Engage in study groups, peer mentoring, and shared flying experiences that make learning collaborative.

Instrument Pilot Requirements

  • 40 Hours of Instrument Time: Fly under the hood in our vintage Cessna 172s, developing the skills to navigate solely by instruments.
  • 50 Hours of Cross-Country Flight: Complete the required cross-country hours as Pilot in Command (PIC), with routes that challenge your navigation and planning abilities.
  • 15 Hours of Instrument Training: Receive focused instruction from our Certified Flight Instructors (CFIs), tailored to your progress and needs.
  • 250 Nautical Mile Cross-Country: Conduct an IFR cross-country flight, covering at least 250 nautical miles and including approaches at three different airports.

How the Club Helps

  • Study Groups: Collaborate with other members to prepare for exams and share practical insights.
  • Mock Checkrides: Participate in practice checkrides with experienced members to fine-tune your skills.
  • Shared Knowledge: Learn from the experiences of others who have recently earned their instrument ratings.
