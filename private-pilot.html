Private Pilot Club

Overview

The Crestwind Aviation Private Pilot Club offers a unique path to earning your Private Pilot License, combining the experience of owning and flying your own vintage aircraft with training in a Cessna 172 equipped with traditional steam gauges. This approach not only enhances your skills as a pilot but also deepens your connection to the aircraft and the art of flying.

Mini Syllabus: Path to a Private Pilot License (PPL)

  • Ground School: Study the fundamentals of flight, including aerodynamics, aircraft systems, and FAA regulations, with a focus on vintage aircraft like the Cessna 172.
  • Pre-Solo Training: Complete at least 20 hours of flight training, focusing on basic maneuvers such as takeoffs, landings, and straight-and-level flight.
  • Solo Flight: Achieve the proficiency required to conduct solo flights after receiving the instructor’s endorsement.
  • Cross-Country Training: Conduct cross-country flight training, learning to navigate using steam gauges and manual controls.

Mini Syllabus (Continued)

  • Night Flight: Perform night flight training, including a cross-country flight and multiple takeoffs and landings to a full stop.
  • Advanced Maneuvers: Refine your skills with instrument training and advanced flight maneuvers.
  • FAA Checkride: Successfully complete the FAA Private Pilot Checkride, demonstrating your proficiency in the aircraft you've trained in.

Membership Benefits

  • Aircraft Ownership: Train in your own aircraft, which deepens your understanding of its operations and builds confidence in its performance.
  • Vintage Aircraft Experience: Gain hands-on experience with traditional steam gauges, manual flaps, trim, and carburetor, providing a tactile and immersive flying experience.
  • Community Support: Engage with a community of fellow aviators who share a passion for flying, aircraft ownership, and the traditions of vintage aviation.
