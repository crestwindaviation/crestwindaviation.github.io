Commercial Pilot Club

Your Path to Becoming a Commercial Pilot

At Crestwind Aviation, our Commercial Pilot Club offers a unique approach to earning your Commercial Pilot License (CPL) by incorporating the hands-on experience of co-owning a vintage, steam gauge aircraft. Here’s how the specific elements of co-ownership help you meet the FAA requirements and set you up for long-term success.

Membership is limited to 8 slots and is available today at $11,000. Contact us at crestwindaviation@gmail.com before they are gone.

Syllabus: How Co-Owning a Vintage Aircraft Supports Your Journey

  • Total Flight Time (250 Hours): Co-owning a plane gives you consistent access to your aircraft, enabling you to steadily build flight hours without the constraints of rental schedules. This steady access allows you to gain diverse experience across various conditions, making you a more versatile pilot.
  • Cross-Country Flights (50 Hours as PIC): The Cessna 172, equipped with steam gauges, offers a hands-on experience that sharpens your navigation skills. Planning and flying cross-country routes in a vintage aircraft requires you to rely on fundamental piloting skills, such as dead reckoning and VOR navigation, ensuring that you master these critical aspects of flying.
  • Instrument Training (10 Hours): Learning to fly instruments on steam gauges builds your precision and situational awareness. The experience of manually scanning instruments and interpreting data prepares you to handle any aircraft’s instrument panel, particularly in complex and challenging flying environments.
  • Advanced Maneuvers: Vintage aircraft like the Cessna 172 provide the perfect platform for practicing advanced maneuvers such as chandelles, lazy eights, and steep spirals. The tactile feedback and handling characteristics of a steam gauge aircraft help you develop a precise touch and deep understanding of aircraft dynamics—skills that are crucial for commercial pilots.
  • Complex Aircraft Transition: While our club’s fleet doesn’t include complex aircraft, the skills you develop from co-owning and flying a vintage plane make the transition to complex aircraft more intuitive. We assist in finding opportunities to log the necessary 10 hours in a complex aircraft, ensuring you’re fully prepared for this aspect of the CPL requirements.
  • Checkride Preparation: The ongoing, hands-on experience of co-owning and flying a steam gauge aircraft builds the confidence and skill required for your checkride. The club environment also provides a network of support, where you can practice with fellow members, gain insights from experienced pilots, and enter your checkride well-prepared.

Conclusion

Co-owning a vintage aircraft with Crestwind Aviation isn’t just about fulfilling FAA requirements—it’s about mastering the art of flying through consistent, hands-on experience. By immersing yourself in the practical realities of aviation, you’ll be better prepared to succeed as a commercial pilot, with skills that are deeply rooted in a strong foundation.
